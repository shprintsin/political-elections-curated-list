# NELDA — codebook (v6.0)

Condensed from the official codebook for search and reference. Authoritative source:
[`NELDA_Codebook_V6.pdf`](NELDA_Codebook_V6.pdf).

## At a glance

- **Unit of observation:** the election round. All rounds of a multi-round election are separate observations (unless reruns occurred in only a small number of districts). ELECTIONID collapses to XXX-YYYY-MMDD-TR (country-year-startdate-type/round).
- **Coverage:** 1945-2020, based on the Gleditsch & Ward List of Independent States. All independent states in existence any time 1945-2020 are covered; countries with no elections at all (e.g. Brunei, Eritrea, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, UAE) are listed but excluded. Microstates (population <500,000 at election time) are included from v5 onward; they were NOT part of NELDA versions 1-4.
- **Inclusion rules (what counts as an election):**
  - Must be for a national executive figure (president) or a national legislative body (parliament, legislature, constituent assembly, or other directly-elected national representative body).
  - Voting must be direct / "by the people" — mass voting takes place. This does not imply universal franchise; a regime may restrict the franchise to a portion of the population and the election still counts.
  - **Indirect elections are excluded** (e.g. Chinese legislative "elections," 1983 Yemen Arab Republic presidential re-election by the Constituent People's Assembly). Presidential elections that use an electoral college (e.g. US, South Korea) ARE included because the college mechanically implements the popular-vote outcome.
  - Special case: Chile 1970 — legislature selected the president from the top two vote-getters per tradition/constitution; included because the indirect legislative choice was determined by the popular vote.
  - Special case: Kenya 1970s — voters elected deputies each pledged to a presidential candidate, with the presidential winner confirmed by a majority of elected MPs; coded as TWO separate direct-election events (legislative + presidential).
  - Special case: Oman — citizens vote for candidates, top 3 vote-getters per district forwarded to the Sultan who makes the final choice; treated as direct because the top vote-getter is nearly always chosen (borderline).
  - **By-elections are excluded** unless they take the form of midterm elections occurring on a pre-established schedule.
  - **Federal systems:** only national-level elections are included (not sub-national/state elections).
  - Elections filling "some small exceptions" — special elections held only to replace suddenly vacated seats — are excluded; otherwise, elections in which a significant portion of a legislative body's seats are filled by voting ARE included.
  - **Referenda are mostly excluded**, with one key exception: referenda that are direct votes on the CONTINUED RULE of an incumbent (functionally equivalent to a presidential election in single-party regimes) ARE included (e.g. the 1988 Pinochet continued-rule referendum). Referenda that merely extend a leader's term but are not leader-specific (e.g. constitutional term-limit changes, referenda outlawing parties) are NOT included (e.g. Equatorial Guinea's 15 Aug 1982 constitutional referendum that also extended the president's term).
  - Elections that are cancelled before/during/after election day are still included IF voting on election day had commenced, regardless of whether balloting was completed, results were announced, or results were respected.
  - Elections may be flawed, uncompetitive, or otherwise irregular and are still included — flawed elections are a deliberate feature of the dataset.
  - **New election vs. new round of an existing election:** decided by (1) whether the regime calls it a new election, and (2) whether candidates must register again / are freely allowed to register. Positive answers to either imply a new election. Example: Ukraine's 2004 presidential rerun of round 2 is coded as round 3 (candidate set carried over from rounds 1-2). Example: Georgia's cancelled Nov 2003 legislative election followed by a March 2004 election with new registration lists/candidates is coded as a new election.
- **Key identifiers:** CCODE (Gleditsch & Ward 3-digit country code) + ELECTIONID (16-character: CCODE-YEAR-MMDD-TR, unique per observation).

## Response values

| Code | Meaning |
|---|---|
| yes | The coded condition holds. |
| no | The coded condition does not hold. |
| unclear | Not enough information to answer — sources are unavailable/sparse, or available sources conflict enough to undermine both "yes" and "no". |
| N/A | Not applicable — the question does not make sense given the election's context (e.g. asking whether the incumbent won when the incumbent did not run). |

Only 3 of the 58 variables allow free-text answers instead of the four values above: `nelda43`, `nelda44`, `nelda54`. Every variable also has a paired free-text note variable for coding clarifications, named `nelda<N>n` (e.g. `nelda1n`).

## Basic fields

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `ccode` | Gleditsch & Ward 3-digit country code. |
| `electionid` | 16-char unique ID: `XXX-YYYY-MMDD-TR` — XXX=ccode, YYYY=year voting started (first round), MMDD=month/day voting started (first round; does not change across rounds), T=office type (P=presidential/direct executive, L=legislative/parliamentary, A=constituent assembly), R=round number (1,2,3…). Bicameral same-day elections are coded as one election. |
| `year` | Year the election ROUND took place (may differ from the year in electionid for later rounds of a multi-round/multi-year election). |
| `type` | Type of national office: Presidential, Legislative/Parliamentary (combined category), or Constituent Assembly. Same-day executive+legislative elections are coded as two separate events (some researchers combine these into "general elections", G). |
| `mmdd` | Month/day the election round took place. For round 1, redundant with electionid. For rounds 2+, gives the actual date of that round (electionid's MMDD only ever reflects round 1). "0000" is used when the month/date cannot be determined. Early/absentee voting does not change this — the official election day is used. |
| `notes` | Free-text field for general remarks, e.g. "massive human rights abuses", "first election in 20 years". |

## Variables

| Variable | Question | Values | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| `nelda1` | Were regular elections suspended before this election? | yes/no/unclear | Asks about suspension in the immediate aftermath of the last round, not whether elections were ever suspended in the country's history. Disbanding the elected legislature counts unless fresh elections follow within about a year. |
| `nelda2` | Were these the first multiparty elections? | yes/no/unclear | First elections of a newly independent country, first multiparty elections after non-democratic rule, or transition from single-party to multiparty elections. Can be "yes" in no-party countries with clear inter-candidate competition (some microstates). |
| `nelda3` | Was opposition allowed? | yes/no/unclear | At least one non-government-affiliated party contesting. If "no" is coded here, `nelda13`/`nelda14`/`nelda15` may be coded N/A. In no-party countries, usually "no" unless well-defined government-vs-opposition candidate groupings exist. |
| `nelda4` | Was more than one party legal? | yes/no/unclear | Legality of multiple parties ≠ existence of a functioning opposition party (a legal opposition party may not exist; an organized opposition may not be legal). No-party countries → "no". |
| `nelda5` | Was there a choice of candidates on the ballot? | yes/no/unclear | Whether voters could choose among candidates (or parties, for closed-list PR) — possible whenever the number of contenders exceeds the number of slots. |
| `nelda6` | If regular, were these elections early or late relative to the scheduled date? | yes/no/N/A/unclear | N/A for non-regular elections lacking a shared expectation of timing. Government-delayed elections without a declared future date can ALSO trigger a "yes" on `nelda1` (functions as a suspension). Coded independently per round for multi-round elections. |
| `nelda7` | Before elections, were there clear indications the incumbent had made a prior decision to give up power (not running / stepping down, incl. constitutional term bans)? | yes/no/N/A/unclear | N/A only when the election doesn't involve the chief executive contest (i.e. when `nelda20`="no"). If the incumbent resigns just before the election and an interim government is installed, the resigned individual is still counted as "the incumbent" unless Archigos recognizes a different leader as installed prior to the election. |
| `nelda8` | Did the incumbent reach his or her term limit? | yes/no/N/A/unclear | N/A if `nelda20`="no" (office not contested). Multi-term systems: "yes" only at the end of the LAST allowed term. Illegal incumbency or no constitutional term limits → "no". Parliamentary systems (leader is an MP) typically unlimited terms → "no". |
| `nelda9` | Had the incumbent extended his or her term/eligibility to run at any point in the past? | yes/no/N/A/unclear | N/A if office not contested (`nelda20`="no") or if there's no term limit system (e.g. most parliamentary systems) → "no" in that case, not N/A. |
| `nelda10` | Was the country ruled by "transitional leadership" tasked with "holding elections"? | yes/no/unclear | Includes caretaker governments, so long as major political actors share the expectation the interim government exists specifically to organize/hold the election (self-declared "transitional" status is not sufficient). |
| `nelda11` | Before elections, are there significant concerns that elections will not be free and fair? | yes/no/unclear | Also coded "yes" whenever the election was widely perceived to lack basic competitive criteria (e.g. only one legal party). |
| `nelda12` | Was the incumbent or ruling party confident of victory before elections? | yes/no/N/A/unclear | Also "yes" if opposition stated it wasn't likely to win, or if the incumbent has been dominant for years and is projected to win a landslide (even absent an explicit confidence statement). N/A when there is no incumbent leader/party (e.g. first election after military rule). |
| `nelda13` | Were opposition leaders prevented from running? | yes/no/N/A/unclear | Distinct from `nelda3`: requires a SPECIFIC opposition party/candidate explicitly blocked. A boycott decided IN RESPONSE to such prevention also counts as "yes". If `nelda3`="no" (no opposition allowed at all) then `nelda13`=N/A. |
| `nelda14` | Did some opposition leaders boycott the election? | yes/no/N/A/unclear | Requires an overt boycott decision by a party (not merely low turnout). N/A if opposition was banned or absent (`nelda3`="no"). |
| `nelda15` | Is there evidence that the government harassed the opposition? | yes/no/N/A/unclear | Includes detaining leaders, disrupting rallies with state forces, shutting down opposition media; police harassment counts. N/A if `nelda3`="no". |
| `nelda16` | In the run-up, were there allegations of media bias favoring the incumbent? | yes/no/unclear | Coded "yes" whenever media is totally state-controlled and/or no opposition is allowed — but a "no" is still possible even in a tightly controlled political system. |
| `nelda17` | Is economic growth in the country said to be good? | yes/no/unclear | Based on PERCEPTION as reported by media, not objective growth statistics — the two need not correspond. |
| `nelda18` | Is the country said to be in an economic crisis? | yes/no/unclear | Perception-based like `nelda17`, not an objective measure. |
| `nelda19` | Is the country said to be a large recipient of outside economic aid? | yes/no/unclear | Aid from other countries or international organizations. |
| `nelda20` | Was the office of the incumbent leader contested in this election? | yes/no | Incumbent leader identity comes from the Archigos dataset (Goemans, Gleditsch, Chiozza), with a few named exceptions (Lebanon, Iran — see `nelda43`). If Archigos names a non-executive figure (king, religious leader) as incumbent, answer is "no". Separately-elected-executive legislative elections → "no" even if held same day as the executive election. First election post-independence or first presidential election → "no". |
| `nelda21` | Did the incumbent run? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if the election doesn't involve the leadership position (e.g. a legislative election under presidentialism). |
| `nelda22` | If no (`nelda21`): was there a chosen successor? | yes/no/N/A | Requires a clearly "anointed"/designated successor understood as the departing incumbent's or ruling group's choice — winning a normal contested primary does not by itself qualify. N/A if incumbent ran, or election doesn't involve the leadership position. |
| `nelda23` | If yes (`nelda22`): did the successor assume power as a result of the election? | yes/no/N/A | N/A unless `nelda22`="yes". Coded only for the outcome of THIS election round in multi-round elections. |
| `nelda24` | Did the incumbent's party lose? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if no party is associated with the incumbent or the regime allows no parties. For legislative elections in presidential systems, refers to whether the incumbent EXECUTIVE's affiliated party lost. If incumbent doesn't run, "yes" is coded if the incumbent's-party candidate loses. |
| `nelda25` | Were there reliable polls indicating the popularity of the ruling party/candidates before elections? | yes/no/unclear | Requires evidence that domestic or international pollsters could actually carry out and publish survey results (a formal ban on publishing results → "no", even if informal observer predictions exist). |
| `nelda26` | If yes (`nelda25`): were they favorable for the incumbent? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if `nelda25`="no". Dead-heat/neck-and-neck polling → coded "no" (not favorable). "Incumbent" here = the leader if the leader's office is contested, otherwise the ruling party/parties in a contested legislature. |
| `nelda27` | Was the vote count a gain for the opposition? | yes/no/N/A | Judged relative to the previous election, not to victory itself — an opposition party can gain without winning. For presidential races, judged by victory/defeat across the two elections, not vote share change. First contested elections → "yes" if opposition wins any seats/representation. Multi-round elections: compare the OVERALL outcome to the PREVIOUS election (not round-to-round), except ambiguous 1st/interim rounds get N/A. N/A if opposition is banned/absent. |
| `nelda28` | Is there evidence that reports critical of the government's handling of the election reached large numbers of people? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if there are no critical reports at all (e.g. election considered free and fair). "No" applies even where criticism exists but is suppressed from reaching a wide domestic audience (e.g. tightly censored regimes). |
| `nelda29` | Were there riots and protests after the election? | yes/no/unclear | Must be at least somewhat related to the handling or outcome of the election (unrelated riots, e.g. pure food riots, do not count). |
| `nelda30` | If yes (`nelda29`): did they involve allegations of vote fraud? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if `nelda29`="no". Multi-round elections: coded only for the outcome of this round. |
| `nelda31` | If yes (`nelda29`): did the government use violence against demonstrators? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if `nelda29`="no". Violence not tied to government actors → "no". |
| `nelda32` | Were results that did NOT favor the incumbent canceled? | yes/no | "Favorable results" judged on how the incumbent (or incumbent-favored candidate/party) performed. Unfavorable + canceled → "yes"; unfavorable + not canceled → "no"; favorable results (canceled or not) → "no". |
| `nelda33` | Was there significant violence involving civilian deaths immediately before, during, or after the election? | yes/no/unclear | Deaths must be at least plausibly election-related. Civil-war deaths not intended to influence, and not caused by, the election should NOT be counted. "Significant" is judged relatively (e.g. ~50 deaths counted as significant in one case). |
| `nelda34` | Were results favorable to the incumbent canceled? | yes/no/N/A | Mirror of `nelda32`. Favorable + canceled → "yes"; favorable + not canceled → "no". For PARTIAL overturns, "yes" only if the overturned portion is significant/substantial enough to tip the power balance; minor/local overturns → "no". |
| `nelda35` | If yes (`nelda34`): was this in part a result of wide-spread protests? | yes/no/N/A/unclear | N/A if `nelda34`="no"; "unclear" if `nelda34` was "unclear". |
| `nelda36` | If yes (`nelda34`): was this in part a result of outside pressure? | yes/no/N/A/unclear | N/A if `nelda34`="no"; "unclear" if `nelda34` was "unclear". |
| `nelda37` | If yes (`nelda34`): was a new election held? | yes/no/N/A/unclear | N/A if `nelda34`="no"; "unclear" if `nelda34` was "unclear". A PARTIAL rerun (e.g. only some polling stations) → "no", not "yes". |
| `nelda38` | If yes (`nelda37`): did victory go to a different party/candidate than at the "initial" stage? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if `nelda37` was "no" or N/A. |
| `nelda39` | Was the incumbent replaced? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if the election doesn't involve the leadership position. "Yes" even if the election itself didn't involve leadership, as long as it triggered events (protests etc.) that dislodged the leader. Incumbent identity per Archigos. If the change happens "soon" after the election but isn't a consequence of the election/related protests, coded "no". |
| `nelda40` | If yes (`nelda39`): did the leader step down because the vote count gave victory to some other political actor? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if `nelda39` is "no" or N/A. |
| `nelda41` | If yes (`nelda39`): was the leader replaced as a result of widespread protests? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if `nelda39` is "no" or N/A. |
| `nelda42` | If yes (`nelda39`): was there a coup that prevented the elected leader from taking office? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if `nelda39` is "no" or N/A. A coup = sources call it a coup, or a sudden, forcible, illegal removal of a government (usually military). |
| `nelda43` | What was the name of the leader in office BEFORE the election? | free text | Per Archigos, with a few named exceptions: Lebanon 1960-2006 (Archigos codes the president, but NELDA treats the PM-selecting-parliament system as making the incumbency contested in all Lebanese parliamentary elections) and Iran (Archigos codes the Ayatollah as executive from 1980; NELDA instead uses the president for incumbent-related questions). |
| `nelda44` | What was the name of the leader in office AFTER the election? | free text | Same name as `nelda43` if no change. Reflects change caused BY the election. For multi-round elections, reflects who is in power after each round (so may show no change until the final round). Same special cases as `nelda43`. |
| `nelda45` | Were international monitors present? | yes/no/unclear | Only OFFICIAL foreign observer delegations (invited by the host government, organized/sponsored by international orgs or INGOs) count — journalists, foreign nationals, or embassy staff giving personal accounts do NOT count. Pre-1990, absence of any mention should generally default to "no" (international monitoring was rare then); in the post-1990 period, sparse sources → "unclear". |
| `nelda46` | If yes (`nelda45`): were Western monitors present? | yes/no/N/A | Western = OECD member countries/organizations. N/A if `nelda45`="no". |
| `nelda47` | If yes (`nelda46`): were there allegations by Western monitors of significant vote-fraud? | yes/no/N/A | N/A if no Western/international monitors. "Fraud" is broad — includes vote-buying and any allegations of significant manipulation undermining credibility; explicit use of the word "fraud" is not required, diplomatic language expressing serious concern suffices. |
| `nelda48` | Were some monitors denied the opportunity to be present by the government holding elections? | yes/no | "No" even if monitors simply didn't attend, as long as the government posed no obstacle. |
| `nelda49` | Did any monitors refuse to go to an election because they believed it would not be free and fair? | yes/no | "No" if there's no evidence any international monitors even considered attending. |
| `nelda50` | Is the country said to be in good relations with the US before the elections? | yes/no/unclear | "Good" = a working, non-antagonistic relationship or better. No evidence of any substantial relationship → "no", UNLESS sources are very poor, in which case "unclear". |
| `nelda51` | Is there a negative change in relations with the US after the election? | yes/no | Requires that relations (good or bad) existed before the election; negative change must follow. |
| `nelda52` | Is there a positive change in relations with the US after the election? | yes/no | Change must be connected to the election/surrounding events, not incidental. |
| `nelda53` | Is the country said to have a substantial economic, military or political relationship with a Western country or Western IGO? | yes/no | "Western" = OECD membership. Only organizations providing substantial economic/military benefit count (World Bank, IMF qualify; the UN as a whole does not, though a powerful specialized UN body might). |
| `nelda54` | If yes (`nelda53`): which one? | free text | N/A (as a value) if `nelda53` is not "yes". |
| `nelda55` | Is there a negative change in the country's economic/military/political relationship with a Western country or IGO after the election? | yes/no | Coded regardless of whether the underlying relationship is "substantial" per `nelda53` — what matters is a negative change in the relationship, however small, causally tied to the election. |
| `nelda56` | Is there a positive change in the country's economic/military/political relationship with a Western country or IGO after the election? | yes/no | Analogous to `nelda55`; coded even if the relationship is not "substantial" per `nelda53`. |
| `nelda57` | Is aid cut-off, or threatened to be cut-off, by an outside actor at any point before or after the election? | yes/no | Outside actor can be Western or non-Western. Threat/cutoff unrelated to the election → "no". |
| `nelda58` | Did an outside actor attempt to influence the outcome of the election by making threats to withhold, or by withholding, something of value to the country? | yes/no | "Something of value" includes aid, IO membership/benefits, trade relations, investment, diplomatic relations, or other external support. Implicit threats count (e.g. the USSR's posture toward Eastern European states 1945-1960). Includes both threats meant to improve election quality and threats conditioned on a particular party/candidate's win/loss. |

## Notes and gotchas

- **Response-value semantics:** "unclear" ≠ missing data by fiat — it specifically means sources are unavailable/sparse OR available sources conflict enough to undermine both "yes" and "no". "N/A" means the question is logically inapplicable given the election's context (e.g. asking about incumbent victory when the incumbent didn't run), not "unknown."
- **Note-field convention:** every `neldaN` has a paired free-text clarification variable `neldaNn` (e.g. the note for `nelda1` is `nelda1n`). Only `nelda43`, `nelda44`, `nelda54` are themselves free-text-valued (not yes/no/N/A/unclear).
- **Round handling in multi-round elections:** each round is a SEPARATE observation; results from the very last/final round are NEVER coded into the previous round's variables — each round's coding reflects only the status of the election AT THAT POINT. Example given in the codebook: `nelda39` ("was the incumbent replaced?") should almost always be "no" until the LAST round, unless the incumbent was formally succeeded before the round's conclusion. Some variables (e.g. government harassment of opposition) are unlikely to change much between rounds but the rules still allow it. When aggregating across rounds, check whether a variable is meant to vary by round or is more likely stable — the codebook flags this per-variable pattern is a common researcher trap.
- **New election vs. continuation round:** decided by (1) does the regime call it a new election, and (2) must/may candidates register again — positive answers to either imply a NEW election (new electionid), not a new round of the same one.
- **Indirect elections excluded** — this is the single most consequential inclusion rule. Also excluded: pure by-elections (unless on a regular midterm schedule), sub-national elections in federal systems, and vacancy-filling special elections for a small number of seats.
- **Referenda**: excluded EXCEPT direct up/down votes on an incumbent's continued rule (treated as functionally equivalent to a presidential election). Referenda that are leader-INDIFFERENT (term-limit changes, banning parties, adopting a new constitution) are excluded even if they incidentally extend a leader's term.
- **Cancelled elections still count** as long as voting had commenced on election day, regardless of whether the count finished, results were announced, or results were honored.
- **Countries with an incumbent identity subtlety:** Archigos is the default source for "who is the incumbent," but NELDA deliberately overrides it for Lebanon (1960-2006: codes around the president despite the PM being the more meaningful "incumbent" for contestation purposes — NELDA still treats the incumbency as contested in every parliamentary election because parliament picks the president) and Iran (Archigos treats the Ayatollah as executive from 1980 onward; NELDA instead follows the elected president for all incumbent-related questions). Also watch Greece 1964 (`nelda10`/`nelda43`): a non-political caretaker PM headed the government, but the "incumbent" for coding purposes is treated as the Centre Union party and its leader, not the caretaker.
- **Coverage:** the country list follows Gleditsch & Ward's List of Independent States, all states existing at any point 1945-2020. Countries with NO elections at all during their existence are excluded outright (Brunei, Eritrea, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, UAE — "no elections" annotated); China is annotated "(indirect national elections)" and excluded on that basis; Zanzibar 1963-1964 had no elections during its brief independence.
- **Inter-coder reliability:** ~20% of country-years were double-coded (duplicates disguised from coders), N=1,195 duplicate elections. Overall agreement is high for structural/factual variables (e.g. `nelda5` choice-of-candidates 97%, `nelda34` cancel-favorable-results 98%) and markedly lower for interpretive/perception variables (e.g. `nelda12` incumbent confidence 62%, `nelda28` reports-reached-large-audience 57%, `nelda17` economic-growth-perception 66%). Treat low-agreement variables with more caution when using them as hard signals.
- **Citation requirement:** if using NELDA, cite Susan D. Hyde and Nikolay Marinov, 2012, "Which Elections can be Lost?" *Political Analysis*, 20(2), 191-210, plus this codebook version.
- **No pages were unreadable** — the entire 38-page PDF (title/TOC, overview, all 58 variable definitions, basic-field definitions, countries-covered list, inter-coder-reliability summary) was read in full.
